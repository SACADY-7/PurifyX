India’s Air Quality Is Getting Worse

Let's understand the challenges and explore smart purification systems.

What is the Air Quality Index (AQI)?

The AQI indicates how polluted the air is. It ranges from 0 to 500, divided into these categories:

Good (0–50)
Moderate (51–100)
Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups (101–150)
Unhealthy (151–200)
Very Unhealthy (201–300)
Hazardous (301+)

Pollution Problems in India (2025)

  • Rapid urbanization and vehicle emissions increasing PM2.5 levels
  • Industrial waste and factory smoke polluting major cities
  • Crop burning in states like Punjab & Haryana
  • Construction dust contributing to suspended particles
  • In winter, smog traps pollutants close to the ground
  • Indoor pollution from cooking, smoke, chemicals & allergens

Why Do We Need Air Purifiers?

Air purifiers help in removing harmful pollutants like dust, PM2.5, bacteria, smoke, and toxic gases.

  • Reduces respiratory problems, allergies & asthma
  • Removes indoor smoke, dust particles, and pathogens
  • Keeps air clean during smog seasons
  • Protects children, elderly, and sensitive individuals
  • Improves sleep, immunity, and oxygen intake
